Professional and personal applications of hypothesizing

  • What happens if we begin to approach or life as a series of hypotheses, as a(n informal) data gathering mission?

If we believe in science, then we believe in something akin to truth. Insofar as we believe that we can move toward truth, every question in life should be framed as a hypothesis to test. How else can we know if we are headed in the right direction?

There are several key elements to a hypothesis, a guess about how the world works. First, it is a proposed statement of fact - this is how I believe the world works, this is the pattern I expect to see in data, if A goes up, B goes down. It must be explicit and precise. Contained. Next, it must be testable, either directly or indirectly. In other words, you must be able to observe something that either sports or reviews your hypothesis.
